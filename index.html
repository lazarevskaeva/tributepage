Martin Luther King Jr.

The Man Who Dreamed of Justice, and Lived to Inspire.

Timeline

  • 1929: Martin Luther King, Jr. is born on January 15 in Atlanta, Georgia.
  • 1941: King's maternal grandmother dies, leading him to attempt suicide at age 12.
  • 1944: At age 15, King enters Morehouse College in Atlanta.
  • 1948: King graduates from Morehouse College and decides to enter the ministry.
  • 1951: King earns a Bachelor of Divinity degree from Crozer Theological Seminary.
  • 1955: King receives his doctorate from Boston University and begins his pastoral role at Dexter Avenue Baptist Church in Montgomery, Alabama. The Montgomery Bus Boycott begins in December, sparked by Rosa Parks' arrest, with King as a leader.
  • 1957: King helps found the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC).
  • 1960: King moves to Atlanta to become co-pastor of Ebenezer Baptist Church with his father and devotes more time to the civil rights movement.
  • 1963: King is arrested in Birmingham, Alabama, and writes the "Letter from Birmingham Jail." On August 28, he delivers his famous "I Have a Dream" speech during the March on Washington.
  • 1964: King is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize. The Civil Rights Act of 1964 is signed into law.
  • 1965: King leads the Selma to Montgomery marches, which contribute to the passage of the Voting Rights Act of 1965.
  • 1966: King initiates a drive against racial discrimination in housing in Chicago.
  • 1967: King publicly opposes the Vietnam War and begins planning the Poor People's Campaign.
  • 1968: King is assassinated on April 4 in Memphis, Tennessee, while supporting a sanitation workers' strike.
  • 1983: The U.S. Congress passes legislation establishing Martin Luther King, Jr. Day as a national holiday, to be celebrated on the third Monday in January starting in 1986.
  • 2000: Plans for the Martin Luther King, Jr. Memorial in Washington, D.C. are approved.
  • 2011: The Martin Luther King, Jr. Memorial is dedicated in Washington, D.C. on August 28.
